Living Well with Dementia

Practical reassurance for families supporting someone living with dementia.

A dementia diagnosis changes things, but people can and do live well with the right support, routine and understanding. This guide shares what helps.

What to expect

Dementia affects everyone differently and tends to change over time. Memory, communication, mood and everyday skills can all be affected. Good care focuses on the person, not the condition — their history, routines and the little things that make a day feel right.

Small things that make a big difference

  • Keep routines familiar — predictability is reassuring.
  • Use simple, calm communication — short sentences, plenty of time, a warm tone.
  • Reduce noise and clutter — calm surroundings lower anxiety.
  • Use reminders — clocks, labels, photos and familiar objects.
  • Focus on feelings — if facts are confused, respond to the emotion behind them.
  • Celebrate what they can still do — independence builds confidence.

How a good care home helps

  • Teams trained specifically in dementia care
  • Calm, easy-to-navigate, dementia-friendly surroundings
  • Reminiscence, music and sensory activities
  • Consistent, familiar carers who learn each person's story
  • Support and a named contact for families, on the harder days too

Looking after yourself, too

Caring for someone with dementia is demanding. Accept help, take breaks (respite care can give you both a rest), and lean on support networks. You matter as much as the person you care for.
