Making the Move Easier

How to help a loved one settle in and feel at home quickly.

Moving into a care home is a big change. A little preparation, and plenty of familiar comforts, can make the transition far smoother for everyone.

Before the move

  • Visit together beforehand so the home feels familiar on day one.
  • Share your loved one's story with the team — routines, likes, dislikes, what makes them smile.
  • Consider a short respite stay first, if that would help them decide.
  • Label clothing and belongings clearly.

What to bring

  • Comfortable, easy-to-wash clothing and sturdy footwear
  • Favourite photos, a familiar blanket or cushion, treasured keepsakes
  • A favourite chair or small piece of furniture, where space allows
  • Toiletries and any glasses, hearing aids or mobility aids
  • Medication and a list of prescriptions
  • Books, music, hobbies and anything that brings comfort and joy

The first few weeks

  • Visit regularly, but allow time for new routines to take root.
  • Join in — a meal, an activity or an outing helps your loved one settle.
  • Keep in touch with the named contact at the home and share any concerns early.
  • Be patient and kind to yourself — mixed emotions are completely normal.
Settling in takes time — often a few weeks. Our teams keep families closely involved every step of the way, and you are always welcome to visit.

Staying involved

You remain a vital part of your loved one's life and care. Open visiting, family events and a team that picks up the phone mean you stay connected and reassured.
